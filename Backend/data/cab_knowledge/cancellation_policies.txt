CANCELLATION POLICIES — RULES, FEES, AND PENALTIES
====================================================

1. OVERVIEW OF CANCELLATION POLICIES
   - Cancellation policies exist to protect both riders and drivers from last-minute disruptions.
   - The policies balance flexibility for riders with fair compensation for drivers' time and effort.
   - Policies may vary by ride category, region, and platform.

2. FREE CANCELLATION WINDOW
   - Most platforms offer a free cancellation window immediately after booking.
   - This window is typically 1 to 5 minutes after the ride is confirmed.
   - During this period, the rider can cancel without any charges.
   - Free cancellation is designed for accidental bookings or quick changes of plans.

3. CANCELLATION FEES
   - If the rider cancels after the free cancellation window, a cancellation fee may apply.
   - Typical cancellation fees range from a small fixed amount to a percentage of the estimated fare.
   - The fee compensates the driver for the time and fuel spent traveling toward the pickup location.
   - The cancellation fee is charged to the rider's payment method on file.

4. WHEN CANCELLATION FEES APPLY
   - After the free cancellation window has expired.
   - When the driver has already started traveling toward the pickup point.
   - When the driver has arrived at the pickup location and the rider does not show up (no-show).
   - For no-shows, a higher fee or the full fare may be charged depending on the platform.

5. WHEN CANCELLATION FEES ARE WAIVED
   - If the driver's estimated time of arrival (ETA) is significantly longer than originally displayed.
   - If the driver is not moving toward the pickup location for an extended period.
   - If the driver asks the rider to cancel (the rider should report this to get the fee waived).
   - If there is a platform error or technical glitch causing the issue.
   - If the assigned vehicle does not match the details shown in the app.

6. DRIVER-SIDE CANCELLATION
   - Drivers can also cancel rides after accepting them, though this is discouraged.
   - If a driver cancels, the rider is not charged and the platform automatically searches for a new driver.
   - Frequent cancellations by drivers result in penalties:
     • Reduced ride assignment priority.
     • Temporary suspension from the platform.
     • Permanent deactivation for excessive cancellations.
   - Valid reasons for driver cancellation include safety concerns, vehicle breakdown, or rider no-show at pickup.

7. NO-SHOW POLICY
   - A "no-show" occurs when the driver arrives at the pickup location but the rider does not appear.
   - The driver typically waits for 3 to 5 minutes after arriving.
   - If the rider does not board the vehicle within the waiting period, the driver can cancel the ride.
   - The rider is charged a no-show fee, which may be higher than a standard cancellation fee.
   - Some platforms also charge a per-minute waiting fee beyond the free waiting period.

8. CANCELLATION OF SCHEDULED RIDES
   - Scheduled rides can usually be cancelled for free up to 30 to 60 minutes before the pickup time.
   - Cancelling within the final 30 minutes may incur a cancellation fee.
   - Once a driver has been assigned to a scheduled ride, standard cancellation policies apply.

9. REFUND PROCESS FOR CANCELLATIONS
   - If a cancellation fee is charged in error, riders can dispute it through the app's help center.
   - Refunds are typically processed within 3 to 7 business days.
   - Refunds are credited back to the original payment method or added as app credits.

10. IMPACT OF FREQUENT CANCELLATIONS BY RIDERS
    - Riders who frequently cancel rides may receive warnings from the platform.
    - Excessive cancellations may result in temporary restrictions on booking.
    - This policy prevents abuse of the cancellation system and protects driver earnings.
    - Platforms track cancellation rates and may flag accounts with unusually high cancellation ratios.
